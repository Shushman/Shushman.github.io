Shushman Choudhury

About Research Resume Miscellaneous

I am a researcher with expertise in intelligent decision-making for transportation and logistics. I currently work as a Lead Research Scientist at Lacuna, where I develop algorithms and digital tools for real-time transportation policies such as curb management or traffic control. Please peruse these blog posts to know more about some of my work at Lacuna: inferring and mitigating airport congestion, and managing the curbside dynamically. I have also represented Lacuna at numerous invited talks for both industry and academic audiences (please see the Resume tab for details)

I have a Ph.D. in Computer Science (specializing in Artificial Intelligence) from Stanford University. I worked with Prof. Mykel Kochenderfer at the Stanford Intelligent Systems Laboratory and Prof. Jeannette Bohg at the Interactive Perception and Robot Learning Lab. In my Ph.D. research, I developed hierarchical algorithms for coordinated multi-robot networks. My work was motivated by several current and future technologies, e.g, delivery drone routing with ground-based transit vehicles as temporary modes of transportation. Please refer to this BBC Digital Planet episode (about 14 minutes in) or this VentureBeat article if you are interested.

I received an MS from the Robotics Institute of Carnegie Mellon University in 2017. There, I was a member of the Personal Robotics Lab and advised by Prof. Siddhartha Srinivasa . I worked primarily on anytime sampling-based motion planning algorithms that I validated on HERB (the Home Exploring Robotic Butler), the lab's robot and research platform. Prior to joining CMU, I graduated with a B.Tech degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur.

I am the immensely proud younger brother of Sanjiban Choudhury, who has shaped my understanding of research, robotics, and many other more important things.
